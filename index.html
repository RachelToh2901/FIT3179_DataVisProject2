Covid-19 Statistic Analysis

An analysis of Covid-19 statistic across the world from year 2020 to September 2022.

Covid-19 Cases by Population for Each Country by 24 September 2022

The map above illustrates total cases by 1 million population across the world by 24 September 2022. We can see that countries like Cyprus, Gibraltar, San Marino has high ratio of total cases per 1 million population so far of more than 600,000 cases per 1 million population, while Niger, Yemen, North Korea has the lowest ratio of only less than 400 cases per 1 million population. Besides that, Europe seems to have the most countries with high ratio of total cases per 1 million, while Africa seems to have most countries with low ratio compared to other continents.

The graph at the side displays top 50 countries with most cases accumulated by 24 September 2022. Despite some countries having high value for total cases by population, their total cases overall in fact may not be great. This graph displays using absolute value of total cases, different than the map above which shows normalized data. United States of America, India, and France have the most cases among all countries in the world, where United States of America having 96,065,161 cases in total.

New Cases and Deaths from year 2020 to 24 September 2022

The stream graph shows the new cases each continent had per day from year 2020 to 24 September 2022.

The highest amount of cases happened during January 2022, which is around 4 million. Before that, the total amount of cases were only below 1 million. But after March 2022, the number of cases started to decrease gradually and drop till below 1.5 million.

The stream graph shows the new deaths each continent had per day from year 2020 to 24 September 2022.

The highest amount of deaths happened during January 2021, which is around 18,000. However, the number of deaths started to drop after that. After May 2022, the number of deaths dropped till below 4,000 till 24 September 2022.

Life Expectancy Distribution of Each Continent by 24 September 2022

The violin plot displays the distribution of life expectancy of each continent. The long vertical black line in the middle represents interquartile range which are q1 and q3 of the data statistic. Besides, the short horizontal black line represents the median. For each violin shape, the part which has large width is the data point that has higher probability to occur, on the other hand part with smaller width is data that has lower probability to occur.

From the graph, we can see that Europe has the highest distribution of life expectancy compared to other continents, while Africa has the lowest life expectancy distribution, despite that Europe has high ratio of total cases per 1 million and Africa has low ratio of it.

Gross Domestic Product per Capita by Country

The graph shows gross domestic product per capita of each country by 24 September 2022, each categorized into their specific continent. From the graph, it is observed that recently more countries in Asia has the highest gross domestic product per capita. The country that has highest GDP per capita is Qatar in Asia which is around 116,000. Next follow by countries in Europe, North America, Oceania, South America, and lastly Africa.
